### 1. Introduction

*Target: 10-15% of total word count*

**Opening context** (1-2 paragraphs): Establish the broader topic and its significance. Why does this area matter? What is the current state of knowledge or debate?

**Narrowing to the research question** (1 paragraph): Move from the broad context to the specific question your paper addresses. What gap, tension, or problem does your research question target?

**Thesis statement** (1 sentence, prominently placed): State your central argument clearly.

{{thesis_statement}}

**Paper overview** (1 paragraph): Briefly preview how the paper is structured and how the argument will unfold. Avoid mechanical lists ("Chapter 2 discusses..."); instead, show the logical progression of the argument.

---

### 2. Literature Review

*Target: 20-30% of total word count*

Organise by theme, not by source. Each sub-section should address a thematic cluster identified in your literature map.

#### 2.1 {{theme_1_name}}

Synthesise the key sources related to this theme. Show how they relate to each other and to your research question.

#### 2.2 {{theme_2_name}}

Continue synthesis. Note areas of agreement and disagreement among scholars.

#### 2.3 {{theme_3_name}}

Identify the gap or tension in the literature that your thesis addresses. This sub-section should transition naturally into your argument.

---

### 3. Body — Argument Development

*Target: 40-50% of total word count*

#### 3.1 {{argument_1_title}}

**Claim:** State the first supporting argument clearly.
**Evidence:** Present and analyse the evidence supporting this claim.
**Counterargument:** Acknowledge and respond to the strongest objection.
**Link:** Connect this argument to the overall thesis and transition to the next section.

#### 3.2 {{argument_2_title}}

**Claim:** State the second supporting argument.
**Evidence:** Present and analyse supporting evidence.
**Counterargument:** Address objections.
**Link:** Transition to the next section.

#### 3.3 {{argument_3_title}}

**Claim:** State the third supporting argument.
**Evidence:** Present and analyse supporting evidence.
**Counterargument:** Address objections.
**Synthesis:** Show how all three arguments combine to support the thesis.

---

### 4. Conclusion

*Target: 10-15% of total word count*

**Thesis restatement** (1-2 sentences): Restate the thesis in light of the evidence presented. Do not copy the original wording verbatim.

**Argument synthesis** (1-2 paragraphs): Summarise how each supporting argument contributed to the overall case. Emphasise the cumulative weight of the evidence.

**Limitations** (1 paragraph): Acknowledge the limitations of your analysis honestly. What could you not cover? What assumptions did you make?

**Future directions** (1 paragraph): Identify questions that remain unanswered or new questions raised by your analysis.

**Closing statement** (1-2 sentences): End with a strong, memorable final thought that reinforces the significance of your argument.
